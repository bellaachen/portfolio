Case Study 02

Building ACY Connect — FIX API Building ACY Connect — FIX API

從零理解機構交易基礎設施，打造公司的第一個 Institutional Product。 Understanding institutional trading infrastructure from zero — and building the company's first institutional product.

專案背景Background

一個沒有畫面的產品A Product with No Screens

2025 年，公司開始規劃進入 Institutional Trading 市場。 In 2025, the company decided to enter the institutional trading market.

過去公司的產品主要服務零售交易者（Retail Traders），例如 Trading Platform、Web Platform 與 Mobile App；但 CEO 希望公司能開始服務更多機構級客戶，例如 Broker、Liquidity Provider、Quant Fund 等，因此決定打造第一個 FIX API 產品 —— ACY Connect。 Our products had always served retail traders — a trading platform, a web platform, a mobile app. But the CEO wanted to serve institutional clients: brokers, liquidity providers, quant funds. So the company set out to build its first FIX API product — ACY Connect.

這是一個和我過去經驗完全不同的產品。它沒有 Chart，沒有下單畫面，沒有一般交易者熟悉的 UI。它的使用者，是另一套交易系統。這也是我第一次真正接觸 Trading Infrastructure。 It was unlike anything I had worked on. No charts. No order screens. No UI a trader would recognize. Its user is another trading system. This was my first real encounter with trading infrastructure.

起點Starting Point

我的第一個反應，不是開始寫 PRDMy First Reaction Wasn't to Write a PRD

當 CEO 告訴我「我們要開始做 ACY Connect，進入 Institutional Market」，我的第一個反應其實不是開始規劃功能，而是四個問題： When the CEO told me "we're building ACY Connect and entering the institutional market," my first reaction wasn't to plan features. It was four questions:

  • FIX 是什麼？What is FIX?
  • Institutional 和 Retail 到底差在哪？How exactly is institutional different from retail?
  • 為什麼公司想做這個產品？Why does the company want this product?
  • 如果我自己都不了解，又怎麼可能設計出讓客戶理解的產品？If I don't understand it myself, how could I possibly design a product clients can understand?

因此，我沒有急著寫需求，而是先開始理解整個市場。我閱讀 FIX Protocol 文件、研究 Institutional Trading 架構、了解 Broker、Liquidity Provider 與 Institutional Client 的合作方式，也研究 PrimeXM、OneZero、IG 等競品的公開文件與產品設計。另一方面，我也持續和 Tech Lead 討論技術架構，理解 FIX Session、Port、Connection 等概念。 So instead of rushing into requirements, I started by understanding the market. I read the FIX Protocol documentation, studied institutional trading architecture, learned how brokers, liquidity providers, and institutional clients work together, and analyzed the public documentation and product design of PrimeXM, OneZero, and IG. In parallel, I kept discussing the technical architecture with our Tech Lead — FIX sessions, ports, connections.

我的目標不是變成工程師，而是理解足夠多，讓我可以做出正確的產品決策。 My goal wasn't to become an engineer — it was to understand enough to make the right product decisions.
我最大的收穫The Key Insight

FIX 不是神祕的技術，而是一套共同語言FIX Isn't Mysterious Technology — It's a Shared Language

一開始，我一直覺得 FIX 非常複雜。直到真正開始閱讀 FIX Specification，我突然理解一件事情：FIX 並不是一套神祕的技術。它本質上是一套共同遵守的資料格式。每一個欄位都有固定的定義，只要 Client 與 Server 對每一個欄位都有相同的理解，就能完成串接。 At first, FIX felt impossibly complex. Then I actually started reading the FIX specification, and something clicked: FIX is not mysterious technology. At its core, it's a data format everyone agrees to follow. Every field has a fixed definition — as long as client and server share the same understanding of each field, the integration works.

那一刻，我突然理解 PM 在 Infrastructure Product 裡真正的工作：不是設計交易引擎，不是實作 FIX，而是把複雜的技術規則，整理成客戶能理解、工程師能實作的產品。 That was when I understood a PM's real job in an infrastructure product: not designing the trading engine, not implementing FIX — but organizing complex technical rules into a product that clients can understand and engineers can build.
我的角色My Role

一座橋樑A Bridge

在這個專案中，我並沒有負責 FIX Engine 或 Infrastructure 的開發。我的角色更像是一個橋樑。我負責： I wasn't responsible for building the FIX engine or the infrastructure. My role was closer to a bridge. I was responsible for:

  • 理解 CEO 想解決的商業問題Understanding the business problem the CEO wanted to solve
  • 理解工程團隊的技術限制Understanding the engineering team's technical constraints
  • 把複雜的 FIX 概念整理成產品規格Organizing complex FIX concepts into product specifications
  • 設計 Back Office 設定方式Designing the back-office configuration
  • 整理對外 Integration DocumentationStructuring the external integration documentation
  • 確保產品模型符合真正使用 FIX 的客戶Ensuring the product model matched how real FIX clients work
Product Decisions

五個產品決策Five Product Decisions

DECISION 01

將 Engineering Configuration 轉換成 Product ConfigurationTurning Engineering Configuration into Product Configuration

Tech Lead 一開始整理了建立 FIX Account 所需要的技術欄位。但我很快發現：工程師理解的設定，不代表客戶能理解。因此，我開始逐一檢查每一個欄位 — 哪些欄位是真正需要提供給客戶？哪些只是工程內部使用？哪些名稱應該改成商業語言？哪些設定可以重新分組，降低理解成本？ Our Tech Lead first listed the technical fields required to create a FIX account. But I quickly realized: configuration that makes sense to engineers doesn't necessarily make sense to clients. So I went through every field, one by one — which fields do clients genuinely need? Which are internal-only? Which names should be rewritten in business language? Which settings could be regrouped to lower the cost of understanding?

我一方面和 Tech Lead 確認技術需求，一方面和 CEO 確認商業需求，最後重新整理 Back Office 的設定介面。我的目標不是把所有工程設定直接搬到畫面，而是讓客戶知道自己正在設定什麼。 I verified technical needs with the Tech Lead and business needs with the CEO, then restructured the back-office configuration UI. The goal was never to copy every engineering setting onto a screen — it was to make sure clients know what they're configuring.

體會：Lesson: Product Manager 的工作，不只是增加功能，而是把技術翻譯成產品。A PM's job isn't just adding features — it's translating technology into product.
DECISION 02

用 Connectivity 建立產品模型，而不是功能分類Modeling the Product Around Connectivity, Not Features

這是我印象最深刻的一次產品討論。專案初期，我們曾經把 Quote 和 Trade 視為兩個獨立功能，因此也曾考慮做成兩個獨立頁面。但深入研究 FIX Protocol、競品，以及和工程團隊討論後，我發現真正需要管理的並不是功能，而是一組 FIX Connectivity。 This was the product discussion that stuck with me most. Early on, we treated Quote and Trade as two independent features — and considered building them as two separate pages. But after digging into the FIX protocol, competitors, and discussions with engineering, I realized what actually needs to be managed isn't features — it's a FIX Connectivity.

每一組 Connectivity 都包含兩個獨立 Channel：Quote（Market Data）與 Trade（Order Execution）。它們仍然維持獨立設定 — 不同 Port、不同 Connection、不同用途 — 但它們屬於同一組 Connection。 Each Connectivity contains two independent channels: Quote (market data) and Trade (order execution). They stay independently configured — different ports, different connections, different purposes — but they belong to the same connection group.

原本：功能分類Before: by feature
Quote Trade
→
改為：Connectivity 模型After: connectivity model
Connectivity
Quote · Market Data Trade · Order Execution

這樣既保留了工程上的獨立性，也更符合機構客戶管理 FIX Connection 的方式。最後，我們採用了這個產品模型。 This preserved the engineering independence of each channel while matching how institutional clients actually manage FIX connections. We adopted this model.

體會：Lesson: 好的產品，不只是簡化畫面，而是建立符合使用者心智模型（Mental Model）的資訊架構。Good product work isn't simplifying screens — it's building an information architecture that matches the user's mental model.
DECISION 03

建立 Institutional Commission（DPM）的產品設定Defining the Institutional Commission (DPM) Configuration

在串接 Institutional Trading 時，我們開始支援 DPM（Dollar Per Million）佣金模式。演算法本身沒有太大的爭議，真正困難的是，不同團隊對於同一套商業規則的理解並不一致 — 哪些設定需要提供給客戶？Back Office 應該如何呈現？哪些名稱才符合市場慣例？ To support institutional trading we introduced the DPM (Dollar Per Million) commission model. The algorithm itself wasn't controversial. The real difficulty was that different teams understood the same business rules differently — which settings should be exposed to clients? How should the back office present them? Which names match market convention?

我的工作不是重新設計 DPM，而是協助整理商業規則，讓產品、工程與商業團隊都使用一致的語言討論同一套邏輯。 My job wasn't to redesign DPM — it was to organize the business rules so product, engineering, and business teams discussed the same logic in the same language.

DECISION 04

理解 Netting，把交易規則帶進產品設計Understanding Netting — Bringing Trading Rules into Product Design

另一個讓我印象深刻的是 Netting。這個概念最早由工程團隊提出，過去我從來沒有接觸過。因此，我重新理解：Netting 與 Hedging 的差異、Position 如何計算、Commission 如何受到影響、為什麼交易引擎需要這種模式。 Another concept that left a mark was netting. Engineering raised it first, and I had never encountered it. So I worked through it from scratch: how netting differs from hedging, how positions are calculated, how commissions are affected, and why a trading engine needs this mode.

理解之後，我開始把這些交易規則納入產品規劃。雖然交易引擎不是由我設計，但我必須理解它的運作方式，才能確保產品規格與系統行為保持一致。這也是我第一次真正開始從「系統」而不是「畫面」思考產品。 Once I understood it, I folded these trading rules into the product plan. I didn't design the trading engine — but I had to understand how it behaves, so the product spec and system behavior stayed consistent. This was the first time I truly started thinking about products from the system, not the screen.

DECISION 05

建立對外 Integration DocumentationBuilding the External Integration Documentation

對 Institutional Client 而言，Documentation 本身就是產品的一部分。因此，我也參與整理對外 Integration 文件，包含文件架構、欄位定義、名詞一致性、對外用語與 Integration Flow。 For institutional clients, documentation is part of the product. So I helped structure the external integration docs — document architecture, field definitions, terminology consistency, external language, and the integration flow.

我的工作不是撰寫 FIX Protocol，而是讓這份文件真正能幫助客戶完成串接，而不是只有工程師看得懂。 The job wasn't to write the FIX protocol — it was to make the documentation genuinely help clients complete their integration, instead of being readable only by engineers.

Reflection

重要的不是學會 FIX，而是建立理解框架The Point Wasn't Learning FIX — It Was Building a Framework for Understanding

這個專案最大的收穫，不是學會 FIX Protocol，而是重新理解 Product Manager 的角色。一開始，我最大的疑問其實很單純：FIX 是什麼？Institutional 和 Retail 有什麼不同？為什麼公司要做這個產品？如果我自己都不了解，又怎麼可能設計出讓客戶理解的產品？ The biggest takeaway wasn't learning the FIX protocol — it was rethinking the PM's role. My initial questions were simple: What is FIX? How is institutional different from retail? Why is the company building this? And if I don't understand it, how could I design a product clients can understand?

後來我發現，真正重要的並不是把所有技術都學會，而是知道如何建立自己的理解框架。 What I learned is that the point isn't mastering every technology — it's knowing how to build your own framework for understanding.

我的理解框架My framework
  1. 先理解商業目標First, understand the business goal
  2. 再研究產業與競品Then research the industry and competitors
  3. 和工程團隊一起拆解技術概念Break down technical concepts with the engineering team
  4. 最後整理成可以交付的產品Finally, organize it all into a deliverable product

這也成為我之後所有專案的工作方式。無論是 Copy Trading、Trading Analysis，還是 Securities App，我都會先理解整個系統，再開始設計產品。 This became how I work on every project since. Whether it's Copy Trading, Trading Analysis, or the Securities App — I understand the whole system first, then design the product.

我相信，Product Manager 的價值，不是知道所有答案，而是能快速理解陌生且複雜的系統，並把它轉化成不同角色都能理解、實作與使用的產品。 I believe a Product Manager's value isn't knowing all the answers — it's quickly understanding unfamiliar, complex systems and transforming them into products that every role can understand, build, and use.
